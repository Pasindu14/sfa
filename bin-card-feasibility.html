The only 3 things I need from you

1. "Stock Taking" vs "Stock Adjustment" — two columns, one source

SFA records every physical-count correction as a single adjustment movement. Your report has two columns. Are these (a) the same thing shown twice, (b) split by sign (count gains vs losses), or (c) two genuinely different processes (auto stock-take vs manual admin tweak)? If (c), it's a small addition — a "reason" tag on the adjustment.

2. "Dis Return Qty" — what is it, exactly?

In your formula it adds stock back in, alongside "Market Resaleable". What's the difference? (e.g. distributor returning stock to the company, vs returns coming from the market?) Once you define the channel, we map or add it. Today SFA has no separate distributor-return flow.

3. What should "Current Stock" be compared against for Variance?

Important nuance: in SFA, the live stock balance is updated by the same movements the bin card sums — so End Stock and Current Stock would always be equal (Variance = 0) unless "Current Stock" comes from a physical count. Your screenshots show real variances (−8, −80), which means "Current Stock" is a counted/physical figure. Good news: SFA already captures that at stock-take time (StockTakingLine: counted vs system, with a stored variance). We'll point "Current Stock" at the latest physical count so Variance is meaningful.
