Can we see stock per SKU on any past date?

e.g. "How many units of SKU X did distributor Y have on 1 June?"

✅

Yes — and it's easy.

The system already records the running balance after every single stock movement. To get the stock on any past date, you just read the last movement up to that date. No guessing, no recalculation.

Is the data being captured automatically?
Yes — every receipt, sale, free issue, return & adjustment is logged
Can we read "stock as of a date" per SKU?
Yes — it's a single, fast lookup on data we already store
Is it accurate for old dates?
Yes — nothing changes stock without leaving a record
Is there a ready-made screen/report for it?
Not yet — needs a small report to be built (data is ready)

How it works — a real example

Take one SKU at one distributor. Every time stock moves, we save the new balance on that line:

The whole trick: we don't add up history every time. Each movement already saves the resulting balance. So "stock on date X" = the balance on the last movement that happened on or before date X. That's why it's fast and easy — even across thousands of SKUs and years of history.

One thing to confirm: opening (go-live) stock. As long as starting stock was entered through a normal receipt, every past date is 100% accurate. If any opening stock was ever loaded straight into the totals without a movement record, dates before that SKU's first receipt would show 0. Easy to fix by posting opening balances as an "Opening" movement — just worth checking once.
